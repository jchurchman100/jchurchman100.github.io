Blanton Museum of Art E-Shop

Designed an e-commerce website to sell items from the Blanton's gift shop.

Timeframe: 1 week | Tools: Figma

Overview

The Problem

The Blanton Museum of Art currently has no option for selling items from its gift shop online. This can be inconvenient for customers who would like to purchase items remotely and likely limits the revenue coming into the museum.

The Solution

The Blanton needs a simple and efficient website for customers to browse and buy from their gift shop. With this option in place, more people will buy from the gift shop, increasing revenue.

My Role

This was an indepent project, so I handled every aspect of the design process. I was provided with information about a user persona, and from there I conducted a competitive analysis, ideated on user flows, sketched and wireframed potential screens, created a prototype, ran usability tests, and put together some mockups after iterating.

The Process

Research

What's out there now?

I started off my process by looking into currently existing websites to perform a comparative / competitive analysis. There were 2 categories of websites that I looked at to gather the information I needed. The first category was gift shop e-commerce sites for other museums as well as similar retailers like souvenir shops. Second, I analyzed some mainstream e-commerce sites like Amazon.

Gift / Souvenir E-Commerce

I looked at other museums that currently had online gift shops set up. This helped me to see some of the standards for how museums were categorizing their products as well as the layout for their navigations. My sitemap was mostly based on these websites.

Test

Usability Testing

I was able to run usability tests on 5 users in total for my prototype. They were to accomplish 3 simple tasks and answered some general questions about their e-commerce experience.

Scenario:
You recently visited Blanton Musesum of Art and saw some items that you liked but didn’t have time to buy them. You want to check them out on the Blanton website to see if you can order them. You already have an account so logging in isn’t an issue.

Summary:
Most users had very few issues and were able to make it through the tests very quickly. However, a couple issues came up in finding the contact information for one user, and there was some confusion on the rewards points system shown in the checkout process.

Task List:

  • Find a pair of earrings that you saw at the gift shop.
  • Purchase the earrings and apply your rewards points.
  • Check to see if a camera that you saw is on sale, and see what it’s reviews are like.
  • Try to see how you can get in touch with someone at the museum.

Key Takeaways:

  • One user didn’t know where to find contact info, since it was only listed in the page footer. They expected to see it at the top.
  • Confusion on how reward points are applied and how they were changing the total price of the purchase.
  • Overall many people felt the site was very familiar to them.

Next Steps

Going forward with the website, I would like to bring more of the Blanton brand in to make the site feel more like a museum. Because of the time limit I mainly focused on usability and simplicity, while leaving room for visual modifications later. I would also work towards a mobile version of the site of course, since that's an obvious way to get even more people looking at the store.

Conclusion

Overview
This project went by really quickly. It was definitely a lesson in time-boxing and focusing on the MVP. In the end I'm pretty happy with the final product and was able to present something I was proud of.

Challenges
Time! I fell behind really fast in this one and had to spend a lot of late nights catching back up. Fortunately I got so focused in my wireframing sessions that I ended up actually getting really far ahead towards the end of the week. Also, during the scope of the project I was just using Figma for the first time, and wasn't familiar with the component system. This made going back into the project to create high fidelity mockups pretty tedious. I'm glad I managed to pick up the habit of using reusable components at this point.

What I Learned
Since this was my first time using Figma and wireframing as part of the real UX process I learned a lot about wireframing techniques and putting together layouts. This was also my first time putting together a user persona, a site map and user flows. These were all very helpful for guiding my direction in the project.
